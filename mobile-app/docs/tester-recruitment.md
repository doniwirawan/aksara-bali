# Closed-test tester recruitment kit — Aksara Bali

Goal: **≥12 testers opted in and continuously enrolled for 14 days**, who
actually *use* the app. Recruit **14–16** to absorb drop-out.

Before sending anything, get the opt-in link from Play Console
(**Closed testing → Testers → Copy link**). It looks like:

```
https://play.google.com/apps/testing/xyz.doniwirawan.aksara_bali_mobile
```

Replace `<LINK>` below with it.

> **Internal testing link** — <https://play.google.com/apps/internaltest/4700728977787153239>
>
> Installs immediately with no 14-day clock, but the tester's Google account must
> already be on the **internal** testers list in Play Console (max 100 accounts).
> Good for quick demos. It does **not** count toward the 12-testers-for-14-days
> requirement — only opt-ins through the **closed** track link above do.

---

## The two things testers always get wrong

Say both of these in *every* message. They are the entire reason applications fail.

1. **Give me the Gmail address you use on the Play Store.** Not a work email, not
   a Yahoo address. If it isn't the Google account signed into their Play Store,
   the app will never appear for them and you won't find out for two weeks.
2. **Do not leave the test programme, and do not uninstall.** They must stay
   opted in for 14 continuous days. Leaving and rejoining resets their clock to zero.

## Where to find 16 people

Ranked by how fast they actually convert, not by audience size.

| Source | Why it works |
|---|---|
| Friends, family, colleagues | Fastest yes. You only need 16 humans, not a market. |
| Bali / Balinese-culture WhatsApp groups | Directly on-topic; high opt-in rate. |
| Sastra Bali / Sastra Daerah departments (e.g. Udayana) | Students and lecturers are your actual target user. |
| SMA/SMP teachers of Bahasa Bali | Aksara Bali is on the curriculum — they have a real need. |
| Instagram / TikTok Bali-culture accounts | Good reach, lower conversion; post with the link in bio. |
| r/indonesia, r/bali, Balinese Facebook groups | Read each group's self-promo rules first. |
| Bali diaspora groups | Motivated to keep the script alive. |

## Keeping them engaged for 14 days

Play rejects applications where testers enrolled but never opened the app. Budget
three touches across the window — that's enough, and more is annoying.

- **Day 1** — the onboarding message above. Ask them to confirm they got it installed.
- **Day 5** — one specific ask, not a generic "any feedback?":
  > "Sudah sempat coba tab **Kuis**? Penasaran level berapa yang paling susah
  > menurut kamu 😄"
- **Day 9–10** — ship a small update (1.5.2) that fixes something a tester
  actually reported, and tell them:
  > "Update 1.5.2 sudah naik — [fix X] dari masukan kalian. Makasih! 🙏"
  This is the single highest-value action in the whole window: it gives you a
  true answer to *"What did you change based on tester feedback?"* on the
  production application, and it pulls testers back into the app.
- **Day 14** — thank them, and ask them **not** to leave the programme until
  you've been approved.
